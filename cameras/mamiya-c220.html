History

The Mamiya C220 offered the interchangeable lens system in a lighter, more portable body. It sacrificed some features of the C330 but gained portability, making it popular with photographers who valued mobility.

Notable Photographers

Popular with travel and documentary photographers who needed the lens system's flexibility without the C330's weight.

Innovations

  • Lighter body design

Collector Notes

Popular with portrait and wedding photographers. Lighter and simpler than C330.

Buying Tips

The C220 is the lighter, simplified entry to the Mamiya interchangeable-lens TLR system. Here is what to check.

  • Bellows: Bellows condition is critical on every Mamiya C-series body. Open the back, hold the body to a bright light, and inspect carefully for pinholes. Even tiny pinholes cause light leaks on film.
  • Lens mount: Mount a lens and check for lateral play. The lens pair should lock securely with no wobble. A loose mount affects lens alignment and image sharpness.
  • No self-timer: The C220 omits the self-timer found on some other C-series bodies. This is by design, not a defect. If you need a self-timer, look at the C330 instead.
  • Weight advantage: At roughly 1.5kg with the 80mm lens, the C220 is noticeably lighter than the C330 series. This makes it more practical for field and travel use. Handle both before deciding.
  • Shared lens system: All lenses from the Mamiya C-series (C through C330S) are compatible. A body without a lens is not usable. The 80mm f/2.8 is the standard and most common. Budget for a lens if buying body-only.
  • Budget entry: The C220 is typically the most affordable way into the Mamiya interchangeable-lens TLR system. It trades some features (no interchangeable screens, no self-timer) for lower weight and lower price.
