Push & Pull Processing

CineStill 800T is surprisingly versatile when it comes to exposure. The underlying Kodak Vision3 500T emulsion has excellent latitude. In daylight (with the blue cast), rating at EI 200-400 produces better results. Under tungsten light, box speed of 800 is ideal. The film pushes well to EI 1600 and can reach EI 3200 in emergencies.

Character & Personality

Grain

Moderate, cinematic grain that is visible but contributes to the film's aesthetic appeal. The underlying emulsion — Kodak Vision3 500T motion picture film — uses T-GRAIN technology, which keeps the grain more organized than traditional emulsions at this speed. The grain adds to the filmic quality that draws photographers to this unique stock.

Exposure Latitude

Wide — CineStill 800T can be rated from EI 200 to EI 1000 without push processing, retaining highlight and shadow detail across this broad range. When needed, the film can be push processed to EI 3200 with increased contrast and grain. The dynamic range is impressive for a high-speed film.

Color Palette

Tungsten-balanced at 3200K, meaning it is designed for warm artificial lighting. Under tungsten and incandescent light, colors appear natural and well-balanced with rich, cinematic tones. Under daylight (5500K), the film produces a distinctive cool blue/teal cast that many photographers embrace as part of its look. The signature red halation effect — a red/orange glow around bright point light sources — is caused by the removal of the remjet anti-halation backing from the original cinema film. This unpredictable halation is what makes CineStill 800T images instantly recognizable.

Best Suited For

  • Night photography and neon-lit cityscapes
  • Concert and music venue photography
  • Indoor events under tungsten or warm lighting
  • Cinematic-style street photography after dark
  • Any situation where the distinctive halation glow is desired
  • Urban exploration and moody environmental portraits

Development Notes

Standard C-41 processing — safe for any commercial C-41 lab. CineStill 800T can also be developed in ECN-2 chemistry (CineStill's own Cs2 kit) for slightly different results. The remjet anti-halation layer has already been removed, so do not process in standard ECN-2 machines that expect remjet removal. The halation effect is part of the emulsion behavior, not a development artifact — it cannot be removed in processing. Static discharge artifacts (pink sparks or smudges) are more common in dry or cold conditions; advance film slowly to minimize this. When scanning, the orange/red halation can confuse automatic color correction — manual adjustments may be needed.
