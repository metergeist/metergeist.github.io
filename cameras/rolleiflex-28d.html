History

The 2.8D continued the evolution of Rolleiflex's premium line with incremental improvements to the shutter and film transport mechanisms.

Collector Notes

Short production run. No meter but excellent optics.

Buying Tips

The 2.8D is a refined version of the 2.8C with an improved meter. Here is what to check.

  • Selenium meter accuracy: Compare the built-in selenium meter reading against a known-good handheld meter in bright light. If the meter reads more than one stop off, the selenium cell is likely exhausted and cannot be repaired.
  • Shutter accuracy: Fire the shutter at every speed from 1s to 1/500s. Listen for consistent timing and watch for hesitation at slow speeds, which indicates the need for a CLA.
  • Lens elements: Examine both taking and viewing lens elements for cleaning marks, scratches, or swirl patterns from improper cleaning over the decades. Minor cleaning marks are common but heavy ones affect contrast.
  • EV scale coupling: Test the EV coupling system by setting an EV value and confirming aperture and shutter speed move together. Check that the coupling can be disengaged for manual control.
  • Value proposition: The 2.8D is generally more affordable than the 2.8E and 2.8F while sharing the same excellent optics. A serviced 2.8D can be a smart buy for shooters who use an external meter.
