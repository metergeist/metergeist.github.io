History

The original Mamiyaflex C, introduced in 1956, established the interchangeable-lens TLR concept that would define Mamiya's medium format legacy. It was the foundation for a system that would serve professional photographers for nearly four decades.

Innovations

  • Interchangeable lens TLR concept

Collector Notes

Historic first interchangeable lens TLR. Foundation for all later C models.

Buying Tips

The Mamiyaflex C is the rarest and oldest of the C-series. Here is what to look for before buying.

  • Bellows condition: After nearly 70 years, bellows deterioration is almost guaranteed. Hold the body up to a bright light with the back open and look for pinholes. Replacement bellows are difficult to source for this early model.
  • Lens mount: The interchangeable lens mount should click firmly into place with no lateral play. A sloppy mount means worn locking tabs and potential alignment issues.
  • Film advance: The film advance mechanism on these early bodies can feel rough or skip. Wind through a dummy roll and check that the counter advances evenly.
  • Parts scarcity: Spare parts for the original Mamiyaflex C are extremely scarce. Unlike later C-series bodies, few repair techs will take these on. Buy only if mechanically sound or for display purposes.
  • Lens compatibility: All C-series lenses (C through C330S) fit this body, but verify the lens mount is not damaged. A body without a lens is useless unless you already own Mamiya C-series lenses.
  • Practical use: This is more of a collector's camera than a daily shooter. If you want to actually shoot, a C220 or C330 body is a far more practical and repairable choice.
