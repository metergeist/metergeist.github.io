History

The Tele-Rolleiflex was a specialized camera with a 135mm Sonnar lens, offering a longer focal length than any other TLR. Its narrower field of view made it ideal for portraits and candid photography from a distance.

Cultural Significance

Extremely rare and highly collectible — one of the most sought-after TLRs.

Innovations

  • Only production telephoto TLR
  • 135mm Sonnar lens

Collector Notes

Rare telephoto variant. Highly sought after by collectors. Portrait specialist.

Buying Tips

The Tele-Rolleiflex is very rare and very specialized. Approach any purchase with extreme caution.

  • Mechanical inspection: Check every mechanical function carefully — shutter, film advance, focusing, frame counter. Spare parts for the Tele-Rolleiflex are nearly impossible to find, so any mechanical issue could render the camera unusable.
  • Service difficulty: Very few technicians have experience servicing this model, and those who do charge premium rates. Factor potential repair costs into your budget before buying.
  • Buy from a specialist: At this price point, always buy from a reputable specialist dealer who offers a return policy. Avoid private sales unless you can inspect the camera in person and test it with film.
  • Not a first TLR: This is a specialized camera for experienced Rolleiflex users who specifically need the 135mm focal length. If you are new to TLRs, start with a standard 75mm or 80mm model first.
  • Rarity premium: Prices are high due to rarity rather than practical advantage over other medium format options. Make sure you genuinely want the Tele-Rolleiflex experience before committing the funds.
